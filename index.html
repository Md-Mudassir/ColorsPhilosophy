Colors Philosophy

Red-danger, passion, excitement

Red has a number of different contextual associations and in branding can deliver a highly visible punch. It has the ability to rev desire; and not surprisingly when it is the color of fire, danger, and blood on one hand.

Yellow – optimistic, cheerful

Being the color of sunshine, yellow puts a smile on the dial. It is the most visible color from a distance (which is why it’s used for street signs) and communicates cheerfulness, friendliness, joy and energy.

Black – sophisticated, formal, luxurious

Black is to be taken seriously. It represents power, luxury, sophistication, and exclusivity on one hand; and death, evil and mystery on the other.

Orange – fresh, youthful, creative

Blending the warmth of red and the optimism of yellow, orange communicates activity and energy. And of course it’s hard not to go past orange as the color of, well, oranges, and all their vitamin c byproducts, which immediately makes orange feel fresh and healthy.

Green – natural, vitality, prestige,

Green has two very common meanings that are quite paradoxical; one being nature and the environment, and the other being finance and wealth. When it comes to nature, green represents plant life and growth and is consequently used to convey being ‘green’ in the environmental, sustainable, organic, natural sense of the world.

Blue – communicative, trustworthy

Blue is the most universally preferred color, perhaps for its very versatile qualities. It is a favorite color for companies that wish to convey reliability, trustworthiness, and communication .
